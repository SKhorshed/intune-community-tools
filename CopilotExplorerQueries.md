This document provides sample natural-language queries you can use with the Intune Copilot Explorer to discover and act on your Intune data. It is based on Microsoft documentation on the Explorer categories [Explore Intune data with natural language and take action](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/intune/intune-service/copilot/copilot-intune-explorer#:~:text=,explore) and usage patterns ([Explore Intune data with natural language and take action](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/intune/intune-service/copilot/copilot-intune-explorer#:~:text=5%20)). You can copy and paste these queries into Copilot Explorer.  

## App configuration  
- "Show me all devices with a specific Wi-Fi profile or VPN configuration."  
- "Which devices have the 'PowerSettings' configuration profile installed?"  

## Apps  
- "What are the top 5 apps installed across my managed devices?"  
- "List devices with version of Microsoft Edge that is out of date."  

## Compliance  
- "Show me devices that are out of compliance and past their grace period."  
- "Get a summary of compliance status by OS version."  

## Device configuration  
- "Find devices where BitLocker encryption is not enabled."  
- "List devices missing a specific security baseline."  

## Device updates  
- "Which Windows devices are missing the latest quality updates?"  
- "Show me devices with pending macOS updates."  

## Devices  
- "Get devices where encryption method is X and encryption is true."  
- "List devices with less than 10 GB free disk space."  

## Endpoint Privilege Management  
- "Show me all admin elevation events for the past week."  
- "List apps that users requested elevated privileges for most often."  

## Advanced analytics  
- "Summarize trends in compliance failures over the last month."  
- "Identify patterns in app crashes across my device fleet."  

## Taking action  
The Explorer can also help you take actions such as adding devices to groups after evaluating your query results ([Explore Intune data with natural language and take action](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/intune/intune-service/copilot/copilot-intune-explorer#:~:text=5%20)). For example:  

- After running "Show me devices that are out of compliance", you can select **Add to group** to target them with remediation policies.  

---  

Use these samples as starting points and adapt them to suit your environment. The Intune Copilot Explorer parses natural language, so feel free to experiment with phrasing to get the insights you need.
